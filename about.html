Our Story

The Crown was founded by Mr. Constantine Demeris in 1933 in France when he traveled from his home country, Greece to this new and beautiful land.

He started this business by the name of “Le Bel Endroit” which means "The Beautiful Place" in English. This name was later changed to The Crown in 1940 in honor of his best friend Mr. Joseph Crown who died in World War II while fighting for his country, Germany. Slowly with time Mr. Demeris' business grew across the world.

As of today, The Crown has its hotels and resorts in more than 20 countries around the world. The current owner of The Crown Group is Mr. Makroviós Anthropos Demeris, the great grandson of Mr. Demeris. In his leadership The Crown is thriving and trying to make our goal of better world for our future generation come true.

Our Aims


As a respectable organization we believe that no aim could be better than building a nice and sustainable world for the future generations and that is exactly what The Crown Group has been trying to do for several decades now by using various methods which are beneficial for our planet.

We are using solar energy in all of our hotels around the world and we hope to do the same for all our resorts as well in next few years. We also have rainwater harvesting in our every hotel and resorts across the world. Also, under our “Project Poseidon” we are trying to bring many rivers back from the dead.

We also aim to provide affordable and accesible medication to all developing countries. Right now we are also trying to provide Covid vaccines to all countries.

Our Contributions


We have done a lot of things for the society from making water reservoirs in the Cape Town to control the water scarcity to providing free education to children in third world countries like Kenya, Zambia and many more countries around the world. But we think that we can do a lot more for the society and that is why we are relentlessly working towards our goal of making a better and sustainable world.

The Crown foundation was also a founding partner of Garvi, the Vaccination Alliance, created in 2001 to improve immunisation access in third world countries. It has donated more than $4.5B to Garvi, which is currently the key player in distributing Covid vaccines in developing countries.
